04: Rapid Prototyping

What To Do

1. Watch a Fusion 360 tutorial.
2. Model either two kit components or simple household objects.
3. Read What is electricity?, What is a circuit?, and Electric power.

What I Did

For this assignment, my kinetic sculpture was a foam-board plane with lights that has some degree of roll using cams. I really enjoyed making my kinetic sculpture, even though my first idea of a drawing robot didn't really work out (I talk about why later). In a nutshell, I spent a day on the drawing robot, scrapped the idea, spent a day making the plane sculpture, and then spent the last day making this webpage. I also learned a lot of different things when it comes to using the Metro board, coding on Arduino, troubleshooting Arduino code, etc. This was probably my favorite project out of the 3 we have done so far, as it incorporated many different facets of digital fabrication, like making a physical model, writing up your own code, adding whatever you want to your model, troubleshooting your model, and much more.

My idea for what to model on Fusion 360 was to make something had moving parts. I didn't want to make anything too complex, so I decided to make a small cologne bottle. At first, I thought it would be super easy, because I was just planning on making the glass bottle, the spray mechanism as one piece, and the cap. However, I decided that I wanted to show the inner-workings of the spray mechanism as well. This proved to be a huge undertaking, as I got stumped at many different steps. I embeded my final cologne bottle assembly below.
